**Abstract.** The ranked list of what's still thin or unresolved — the fuel for the next `/deepen` pass.
Ordered by leverage: biggest target-minus-current gap and most actionable first.

---

1. **Practice *quality* mechanisms (the #1 gap — L2, target L4).** The whole "quality > quantity" pillar
   ([[principles]] §7, [[disagreements]] D4) rests on theory + convention, because the effect-size
   literature measured *quantity*. **Dig:** studies that operationalize and measure feedback quality,
   error-focus, edge-of-ability difficulty, and mental-representation building as *separate* variables
   from hours. This is the most decision-relevant unknown for a learner.

2. **Replicate the genetic-confounding magnitude ([[mosing-2014]]).** One flagship twin study, one domain
   (music), self-reported practice. **Dig:** the 2024 twin follow-ups; genetically-informative designs in
   *other* domains (sport, chess); objective (non-self-report) practice measures. Would move [[disagreements]]
   D3 from MEDIUM toward HIGH (or flip the magnitude).

3. **Mechanism of the starting-age effect.** [[gobet-campitelli-2007]] shows earlier starters reach more
   at equal hours, but doesn't separate **neural plasticity** from **more cumulative low-intensity
   exposure**. **Dig:** designs that hold total exposure constant while varying start age.

4. **Does practice interact with cognitive ability?** The multifactorial camp ([[hambrick-2014]]) implies
   practice × working-memory / cognitive-ability interactions. **Dig:** whether the *return* on practice
   differs by cognitive profile — directly relevant to who should grind vs. stack other levers.

5. **Generalize the domain gradient.** The 26%→<1% gradient ([[benchmarks]]) is across five domain
   buckets. **Dig:** finer-grained structure metrics — can we predict an *arbitrary* skill's practice-ROI
   from measurable properties (rule stability, feedback latency, environment predictability)?
