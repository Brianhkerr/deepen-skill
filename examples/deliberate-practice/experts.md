**Abstract.** The source map, ranked by the **proxy ladder** — not fame: (1) checkable past calls,
(2) shows work / reproducible, (3) calibration behavior, (4) peer recognition [weak], (0) fame alone
[not evidence]. The rung that earned each weight is recorded. Each entry links to its [[_index|sources]]
digest.

---

## Canon (foundational)

### K. Anders Ericsson (d. 2020) — credibility: **HIGH**, with a caveat
- **Who:** Originator of the deliberate-practice framework ([[ericsson-krampe-teschromer-1993]]); author
  of *Peak* ([[ericsson-pool-peak-2016]]).
- **Rung earned:** 2 (shows work — built the construct from primary data) + 3 (calibration — publicly
  corrected the 10,000-hour misreading rather than ride the fame; [[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]]).
- **Caveat / `[inference]`:** as the construct's author he has a stake in maximizing its explanatory
  share, and his strongest claims (practice as near-sole driver, "no innate limits") are the ones the data
  has pushed back on hardest. Weight his *definition* highly; weight his *effect-size optimism* lower.

### Malcolm Gladwell — credibility: **LOW** (as a science source)
- **Who:** Popularized "10,000 hours" in *Outliers* ([[gladwell-outliers-2008]]).
- **Rung earned:** 0 (fame/reach only). The underlying researcher disowns his interpretation
  ([[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]]). Listed because he's the source of the field's dominant
  *misconception* — useful to name, not to trust.

## Frontier (currently pushing the question)

### Brooke Macnamara — credibility: **HIGH**
- **Who:** Lead author of the field-defining skeptical meta-analysis ([[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]])
  and the pre-registered, better-controlled 2019 revisit of Ericsson's original ([[macnamara-maitra-2019]]).
- **Rung earned:** 1 + 2 + 3 — a pre-registered prediction that aged well, open data, and willingness to
  test her own prior with stronger methods than the original. The strongest methodological track record
  in the dispute. `[inference]`

### David Z. Hambrick — credibility: **HIGH**
- **Who:** Multifactorial-model proponent; co-author on the meta-analysis and the chess/music reanalyses
  ([[hambrick-2014]]).
- **Rung earned:** 2 — argues from converging datasets (chess, music) rather than a single study.

### Fernand Gobet & Guillermo Campitelli — credibility: **HIGH** (chess specifically)
- **Who:** Longitudinal chess-practice data showing the huge hours-to-mastery spread ([[gobet-campitelli-2007]]).
- **Rung earned:** 1 + 2 — their specific, checkable claim (wide spread; some heavily-practiced players
  never master) has held up and is widely cited.

### Miriam Mosing et al. — credibility: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
- **Who:** Behavioral-genetics twin study on practice and music ability ([[mosing-2014]]).
- **Rung earned:** 2 — large genetically-informative sample (n≈10,500). Held at MEDIUM-HIGH not HIGH
  because it's a single flagship design on one domain; `[inference]` the 2024 twin follow-ups (a
  [[frontier]] item) are wanted before treating the gene-mediation magnitude as settled.

## Loud-but-unsubstantiated consensus to flag
The "anyone can master anything with 10,000 hours" self-help genre. High reach, **rung 0**, contradicted
by the primary researcher and the variance data. Naming it is part of the value.
