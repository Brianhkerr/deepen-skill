**Abstract.** The numbers an expert carries in their head, each linked to its [[_index|source]] digest.
These are *measured* findings, not conventions — but they're meta-analytic point estimates, so treat them
as "best current estimate," not physical constants. `decays: slow` (these are stable empirical results,
not fast-moving platform numbers).

| Number | Value | Source | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Variance explained by deliberate practice — **games** | **26%** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] `^variance-by-domain-2014` | Most practice-friendly domain |
| — **music** | **21%** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] | |
| — **sports** | **18%** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] | |
| — **education** | **~4%** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] | Ill-structured → low |
| — **professions** | **< 1%** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] | Least practice-driven |
| Cross-domain average variance explained | **~12%** | [[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]] (reporting the meta-analysis) | The "12%" headline figure |
| Meta-analysis scope | **88 studies, 157 effect sizes, n > 11,000** | [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] | Largest survey at publication |
| Music-practice heritability | **40–70%** | [[mosing-2014]] `^practice-heritable-2014` | How much you practice is itself partly genetic |
| Practice–ability correlation (music) | **r = .18–.36** | [[mosing-2014]] | Modest; and predominantly genetic |
| Chess — minimum DP to reach master | **~3,000 hours** | [[gobet-campitelli-2007]] `^hours-spread-2007` | A floor, not a guarantee |
| Chess — hours-to-mastery spread | **~order of magnitude** across players | [[gobet-campitelli-2007]] | Some heavily-practiced players never master |
| Top musicians — practice by ~age 20 | **~10,000 hr (group average)** | [[ericsson-krampe-teschromer-1993]] | The origin of the "10k" number — an average, not a threshold |
| International competition winners | **~25,000 hr** | [[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]] | Far above 10k — the figure is not constant |

> **Reading the table:** the domain gradient (26% → <1%) is the single most actionable number set — it
> tells you *where* deliberate practice is worth grinding. See [[playbook]] T1 and [[principles]] §3.
