**Abstract.** The proof-filtered, procedural layer: how to invest limited practice time, for the example
persona *an adult deciding how to practice a new skill.* Ranked by **ICE** (Impact × Confidence × Ease) —
`[convention]`: ICE is a prioritization forcing-function, not a measured score. Each tactic names the
principle it rides and its evidence strength. Re-derive if the [[principles]] change.

---

### T1. Pick the domain before trusting the grind `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence H · Ease H → top priority.** Practice's payoff is domain-dependent: large in
stable-rule domains (games, instruments, motor skills), small in ill-structured ones
([[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]]; [[principles]] §3). *Action:* if your target is ill-structured,
expect reps to matter less and stack other levers (feedback sources, mentors, transfer from adjacent
skills). **Metric:** can you state where your domain sits on the structure gradient before committing
1,000 hours?

### T2. Engineer feedback loops, don't just log hours `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence M · Ease M.** The necessary-not-sufficient finding plus the heavily-practiced
non-masters say volume without correction plateaus ([[gobet-campitelli-2007]]; [[principles]] §2).
*Action:* build immediate, specific feedback into every session (coach, recording, solver, test).
**Metric:** % of practice time with a feedback signal attached. Riding the quality-over-quantity thesis —
held at **M confidence** because that pillar is the KB's thin spot ([[disagreements]] D4).

### T3. Practice at the edge of ability, on your weakest sub-skill `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence M · Ease M.** Core to Ericsson's narrow construct: targeted, effortful,
error-focused reps beat comfortable repetition ([[ericsson-krampe-teschromer-1993]]; [[ericsson-pool-peak-2016]]).
*Action:* isolate the specific failing component and drill it, rather than re-performing the whole skill.
**Metric:** are you failing ~15–30% of attempts? Too-easy practice is a tell. (`[convention]` on the exact band.)

### T4. Start sooner; if you can't, compensate with intensity `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence M · Ease L.** Earlier starters reach more at equal hours ([[gobet-campitelli-2007]];
[[principles]] §6). *Action (adult learner):* you can't change start age, so raise per-hour quality
(T2/T3) and protect consistency. **Metric:** sessions/week sustained over months, not a one-off binge.

### T5. Kill the 10,000-hour mental model `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence H · Ease H.** A fixed-hours target is wrong and can demotivate
([[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]]; [[disagreements]] D2). *Action:* set **criterion-referenced** goals
(demonstrable sub-skills), not hour-count goals. **Metric:** your milestones are abilities, not hours.

### T6. Calibrate expectations to genetics without fatalism `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence M · Ease H.** Trainability and even practice-propensity have genetic components
([[mosing-2014]]; [[principles]] §5). *Action:* judge progress against *your own* prior curve, not the
top of the distribution; expect different return rates across people. **Metric:** track personal
rate-of-improvement, not peer comparison. (Direction firm, magnitude uncertain — [[disagreements]] D3.)
