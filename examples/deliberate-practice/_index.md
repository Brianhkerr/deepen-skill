## Scope
What actually determines whether deliberate practice produces expert performance — how much of the
variance it explains, where it breaks down, and what to do about it as a learner. Bounded to the
*skill-acquisition science*; excludes motivation/grit literature except where load-bearing.

## State of the field (one paragraph)
Deliberate practice is **necessary but demonstrably not sufficient**, and the popular "10,000-hour rule"
is a journalistic distortion the original author disowns. The best meta-analytic estimate is that
accumulated practice explains roughly **20–25% of performance variance in games, music, and sports, and
far less in education and the professions** — large, but a minority of the story. The rest is split across
starting age, genetics (including a genetic component to *how much you practice*), working memory, and
practice *quality*. The live fight is about **definitions**, not direction: the skeptics measured a broad
notion of practice; Ericsson says that's not his construct — but the pre-registered 2019 revisit tested
the narrow construct directly and still found a smaller effect.
