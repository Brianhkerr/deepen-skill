## Scope
What actually determines whether deliberate practice produces expert performance — how much of the
variance it explains, where it breaks down, and what to do about it as a learner. Bounded to the
*skill-acquisition science*; excludes motivation/grit literature except where it's load-bearing.

## The one-paragraph state of the field
Deliberate practice is **necessary but demonstrably not sufficient**, and the popular "10,000-hour
rule" is a journalistic distortion that the original author disowns. The best meta-analytic estimate
is that accumulated practice explains roughly **20–25% of performance variance in games, music, and
sports, and far less in education and the professions** — large, but a minority of the story. The
remaining variance is split across starting age, genetics (including a genetic component to *how much
you practice*), working memory, and practice *quality*. The live fight is about **definitions**, not
direction: the skeptics measured a broad notion of practice; Ericsson says that's not his construct.
