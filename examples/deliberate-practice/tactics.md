# Tactics — ranked playbook for a serious learner

Translation of the principles into action, for the example persona: *an adult deciding how to invest
limited practice time in a new skill.* Ranked by **ICE** (Impact × Confidence × Ease) — `[convention]`:
ICE is a prioritization forcing-function, not a measured score. Each tactic notes the principle it
rides and its evidence strength.

> Decay flags: `decays: slow` for mechanisms grounded in the durable findings; this whole file is
> learner-facing convention layered on the science, so re-derive if the principles change.

---

### T1. Pick the domain before trusting the grind `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence H · Ease H → top priority.** Practice's payoff is domain-dependent: large in
stable-rule domains (games, instruments, motor skills), small in ill-structured ones (management,
general "professions") ([Macnamara 2014](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0956797614535810)).
*Action:* if your target is ill-structured, expect reps to matter less and stack other levers (feedback
sources, mentors, transfer from adjacent skills). **Metric:** can you state where your domain sits on
the structure gradient before committing 1,000 hours?

### T2. Engineer feedback loops, don't just log hours `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence M · Ease M.** The necessary-not-sufficient finding plus the >20,000-hour
non-masters say volume without correction plateaus
([Gobet & Campitelli 2007](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S104160800700129X)).
*Action:* build immediate, specific feedback into every session (coach, recording, solver, test). **Metric:**
% of practice time with a feedback signal attached. Riding the quality-over-quantity thesis — held at
**M confidence** because that pillar is the KB's thin spot (see [[disagreements]] D4).

### T3. Practice at the edge of ability, on your weakest sub-skill `decays: slow`
**Impact H · Confidence M · Ease M.** Core to Ericsson's narrow construct: targeted, effortful, error-
focused reps beat comfortable repetition ([Ericsson 1993](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ471947)). *Action:*
isolate the specific failing component and drill it, rather than re-performing the whole skill. **Metric:**
are you failing ~15–30% of attempts? Too-easy practice is a tell. (`[convention]` on the exact band.)

### T4. Start sooner; if you can't, compensate with intensity `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence M · Ease L.** Earlier starters reach more at equal hours
([Gobet & Campitelli 2007](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S104160800700129X)).
*Action (adult learner):* you can't change start age, so raise per-hour quality (T2/T3) and protect
consistency. **Metric:** sessions/week sustained over months, not a one-off binge.

### T5. Kill the 10,000-hour mental model `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence H · Ease H.** A fixed-hours target is wrong and can demotivate
([Smithsonian / Ericsson](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/10000-hour-rule-not-real-180952410/)).
*Action:* set **criterion-referenced** goals (demonstrable sub-skills) not hour-count goals. **Metric:**
your milestones are abilities, not hours.

### T6. Calibrate expectations to genetics without fatalism `decays: slow`
**Impact M · Confidence M · Ease H.** Trainability and even practice-propensity have genetic components
([Mosing 2014](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25079217/)). *Action:* judge progress against *your own*
prior curve, not the top of the distribution; expect different return rates across people. **Metric:**
tracking personal rate-of-improvement, not peer comparison. (Direction firm, magnitude uncertain — D3.)
