**Abstract.** The vocabulary map — terms as insiders use them, which is *also* a retrieval bridge (search
by meaning and by the field's actual words). The deliberate/purposeful/naive distinction is load-bearing:
most popular confusion comes from collapsing it.

- **Deliberate practice** — the narrow construct: effortful, goal-directed activity **designed (usually
  by a teacher / established method)** to improve a specific aspect of performance, with immediate
  feedback and edge-of-ability repetition ([[ericsson-krampe-teschromer-1993]]). *Not* time-on-task.
- **Purposeful practice** — focused practice with goals and feedback but **without** the expert/teacher-
  designed structure. Ericsson's own intermediate category ([[ericsson-pool-peak-2016]]); the [[macnamara-maitra-2019]]
  test that "teacher-designed practice ≈ practice alone" is precisely about this line.
- **Naive practice / mere experience** — repeating an activity at your current level without targeted
  error-focus. Feels like practice; builds little. The thing [[failure-modes]] F2 warns against.
- **The "10,000-hour rule"** — the popular claim that ~10k hours of practice yields mastery
  ([[gladwell-outliers-2008]]). A **misconception**: the number was a group *average*, not a threshold,
  and is disowned by the originating researcher ([[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]]).
- **Mental representations** — the rich, domain-specific internal models that edge-of-ability practice
  builds; Ericsson's proposed *mechanism* for why structured practice works ([[ericsson-pool-peak-2016]]).
- **Necessary but not sufficient** — the KB's headline verdict: you can't reach elite level without large
  practice, but practice alone doesn't get you there ([[principles]] §2).
- **Variance explained (R² / % of variance)** — the share of *between-person* differences in performance
  a factor statistically accounts for. "Practice explains 21% of variance in music" ≠ "practice is 21% of
  skill." It's a population-level association, not an individual recipe.
- **Heritability** — the share of *variation in a trait across a population* attributable to genetic
  variation (e.g. practice amount is 40–70% heritable, [[mosing-2014]]). A population statistic; it does
  **not** mean "70% of your practice is genetic."
- **Monozygotic (MZ) within-pair design** — comparing identical twins who differ on an exposure (here,
  practice) to control for genetics. The "no within-pair difference" result is [[mosing-2014]]'s decisive test.
- **Sensitive period / starting age** — the developmental-window moderator: earlier starters reach more at
  equal hours ([[gobet-campitelli-2007]]); mechanism still open ([[frontier]]).
- **Domain structure gradient** — `[inference]` the observed pattern that practice-ROI tracks how
  well-structured/stable a domain's rules and feedback are (games high → professions low; [[benchmarks]]).
