**Abstract.** The behavioral-genetics result that breaks the clean "practice → skill" causal arrow.
In 10,500 Swedish twins, the practice–ability correlation was **predominantly genetic**, and identical
twins who practiced more were **not** reliably better than their co-twins who practiced less. This is
the KB's basis for [[disagreements]] D3 (genetics routes *through* practice). **Fidelity note:** every
figure below is quoted directly from the PubMed abstract (PMID 25079217).

## Substantive claims (the source's own argument)
1. **Sample.** 10,500 Swedish twins, assessed on music practice and three music-ability tasks (rhythm,
   melody, pitch discrimination).
2. **Practice–ability correlations were modest.** rs = .18–.36 between amount of practice and ability.
3. **Music practice is substantially heritable: 40%–70%.** ^practice-heritable-2014 How much someone
   practices is itself partly genetic — not purely a free choice or environmental input. This is the
   uncomfortable finding for the standard model (practice = environment, talent = genes).
4. **The association is predominantly genetic, not causal.** "Associations between music practice and
   music ability were predominantly genetic, and… nonshared environmental influences did not contribute."
5. **The within-pair null — the decisive test.** "There was no difference in ability within monozygotic
   twin pairs differing in their amount of practice." When genetic predisposition is held constant (by
   comparing identical co-twins), more practice was **no longer** associated with better skill. ^within-mz-null-2014
6. **Authors' conclusion.** Practice "may not causally influence music ability"; genetic variation
   affects **both** ability **and** the inclination to practice. So naive practice–performance
   correlations overstate practice's causal effect.

## How to weight this (calibration)
- Held at **MEDIUM** confidence in [[disagreements]] D3: large n, but a *single* flagship design on a
  *single* domain (music), self-reported practice, one cohort, and a counter-intuitive result that
  deserves replication before the *magnitude* is treated as settled. The *direction* (some genetic
  confounding exists) is held more firmly than the size. Pulling the 2024 twin follow-ups is a
  [[frontier]] item.

## What this note cannot support
- Generalization beyond music; the causal magnitude in other domains; whether the null holds under
  objective (non-self-report) practice measures.
