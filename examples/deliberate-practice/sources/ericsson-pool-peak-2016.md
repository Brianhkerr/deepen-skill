**Abstract.** Ericsson's own general-audience restatement of deliberate-practice theory, two decades
after the 1993 paper. Useful for the *definitions* (it's where the deliberate-vs-purposeful distinction
is cleanest) and as his on-record disavowal of the 10,000-hour threshold. **Fidelity note:** book not
fetched; this digest is limited to Ericsson's positions that are independently corroborated by the
primary record and contemporaneous reporting — it does not quote the book verbatim.

## Substantive claims (the source's argument)
1. **Re-asserts the narrow construct.** Distinguishes **deliberate practice** (purposeful practice that
   is *also* guided by an established field's known methods, usually via a teacher) from **purposeful
   practice** (focused, with goals and feedback, but not expert-designed) and from naive repetition.
   This three-way split feeds the [[glossary]].
2. **Disowns the 10,000-hour "rule."** Restates that the number was an average for one group at one age,
   not a threshold or a guarantee — consistent with [[ericsson-10000-hour-disavowal]].
3. **Mechanism — mental representations.** Argues the engine of improvement is building richer
   domain-specific *mental representations* through edge-of-ability practice with feedback — the
   mechanistic claim behind [[principles]] §7's "quality over quantity" (which the KB still holds at LOW
   confidence, because the effect-size literature measured quantity, not this).
4. **Strong-form optimism (discounted).** Leans toward practice as the near-dominant driver and downplays
   innate limits. [[experts]] weights his *definition* highly but his *effect-size optimism* lower — this
   is exactly the claim [[macnamara-hambrick-oswald-2014]] and [[mosing-2014]] push back on.
