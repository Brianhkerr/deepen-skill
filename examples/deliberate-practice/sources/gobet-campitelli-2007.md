**Abstract.** The cleanest single empirical refutation of any fixed-hours "rule." A longitudinal survey
of chess players found the practice needed to reach master level varies by roughly an order of magnitude
between people — and that some heavily-practiced players never reach it. **Fidelity note:** the design
(104 players, questionnaire) and the headline conclusions are confirmed via the journal record and the
authors' own 2011 review summarizing this study; the paywalled full text was not fetched, so the exact
upper-bound hour figures are reported as the cited literature gives them.

## Substantive claims (the source's own argument)
1. **Design.** Questionnaire study of 104 chess players of differing skill, reporting rating, hours of
   individual and group practice, learning resources, and coaching.
2. **Necessary but not sufficient.** Abundant deliberate practice is *necessary* to reach high skill, but
   does not *guarantee* it — the finding the KB leans on for [[principles]] §2 and [[disagreements]] D1.
3. **Huge between-person spread in hours-to-mastery.** ^hours-spread-2007 The minimum to reach master
   level was estimated around **3,000 hours**, but the range to reach a given level spanned roughly an
   order of magnitude across players; slower players required far more practice for the same level. Some
   players accumulated very large practice totals **without** reaching master. This order-of-magnitude
   spread is the single cleanest disproof of a constant "X hours = mastery" claim ([[disagreements]] D2).
4. **Starting age matters.** Players who started younger reached higher levels at equal accumulated
   practice — evidence for a developmental/sensitive-period moderator (see [[principles]] §6). [inference
   on mechanism: the study identifies the age effect but does not cleanly separate plasticity from
   cumulative low-intensity exposure.]
5. **Other factors contribute.** The authors emphasize cognitive ability, handedness, and season/age of
   starting as additional contributors — a multifactorial picture consistent with [[hambrick-2014]].

## What this note cannot support
- The exact maximum-hours figures for non-master players (the KB's "~3,000 to >23,000" range is drawn
  from this longitudinal data as reported in the secondary literature; the precise upper bound was not
  re-verified against the paywalled tables). The *direction and order-of-magnitude* are solid; treat the
  exact ceiling as approximate.
